What is E-Waste?

Electronic waste (e-waste) refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices. Used electronics which are destined for refurbishment, reuse, resale, salvage recycling through material recovery, or disposal are also considered e-waste.

Improper disposal of e-waste leads to environmental pollution and health hazards due to toxic substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium leaching into soil and water.

The Impact of E-Waste

Environmental Damage

Toxic chemicals from e-waste leach into soil and water, contaminating ecosystems and food chains.

Health Risks

Exposure to e-waste can cause respiratory issues, skin disorders, and even cancer.

Resource Waste

Valuable materials like gold, silver, and copper are lost when electronics aren't recycled.

What You Can Do

Proper Recycling

Find certified e-waste recyclers in your area who follow environmentally sound practices.

Working electronics can be donated to schools, nonprofits, or sold to refurbishers.

Buy Responsibly

Choose electronics with longer lifespans and from companies with take-back programs.
